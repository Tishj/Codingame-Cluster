This is a league based challenge.

For this challenge, multiple leagues for the same game are available. Once you have proven yourself against the first Boss, you will access a higher league and extra rules will be available.

Tutorial video by Gaurav Sen: https://youtu.be/gZMdOiqchDk

  Goal

Connect enough chips to win before your opponent.
Decide between placing chips at strategic positions, or to rotate the board to a different side.

  Rules

The game takes place on a hexagonal grid representing the galaxy.
The players take turns to take actions.

Galaxy

The galaxy is made up of 37 hexagonal cells, arranged to form a larger hexagon.
Each cell has an index and up to six neighbors. Each direction is labelled 0 to 5 and have a one-to-one relation with gravity.
Hex directions

The distance between two cells equals the minimum number of cells to go through to get from one to the other.
Each cell may contain a chip. Each chip is owned by one of the players.

Chips

Every player has a bag of their chip color, at the start of each round 2 chips are selected from this bag.
At the start of a turn, the player receives chips of a certain color from their bag.
Then the player can place one of these chips on the board.
When making a DROP action, the other selected chips go back into the bag.
When a ROTATE action is made, the selected chips are destroyed.

Chips are affected by gravity and will fall until they hit another chip or the board boundary.

Gravity

The board is rotated to a side, corresponding to the gravity:
  • Side 0 the board is leaning on its SOUTH side.
  • Side 1 the board is leaning on its SOUTH WEST side.
  • Side 2 the board is leaning on its NORTH WEST side.
  • Side 3 the board is leaning on its NORTH side.
  • Side 4 the board is leaning on its NORTH EAST side.
  • Side 5 the board is leaning on its SOUTH EAST side.
The direction of a shadow depends on which direction the sun is currently pointing towards.
On day 0, the sun is pointing towards direction 0, meaning all shadows are being cast to the right.
ROTATE commands can be used to change the gravity by cycleAmount, coming back to 0 after passing 5.

Actions

The players take turns performing actions, the player can perform one of two possible actions.
The possible actions are:
  • DROP column color: Insert a chip of a specific color at the top of the board, at a specific column.
  • ROTATE amount: Rotate the board clock-wise by a certain amount of cycles (1-5), changing the gravity.

Drop action

When performing a drop action, you reduce your amount of remaining chips for that color by 1.
The other selected chips go back into the bag.
You may not drop onto a column that is currently filled.
You can only drop a color that has been selected for you for that round.

Rotate action

When performing a rotate action, you destroy all of the chips that were selected for you at the start of the round. (reducing the amount remaining)
You may only rotate by 1-5(inclusive) cycles.
Chips will fall according to this new gravity

‚õî Game end

The game lasts at most 34 rounds or until one player has made a connection of 4 or more, and bigger than their opponent.
If players have a connection of the same length, the winner is decided by the color of the connection.
The color with the least amount of chips of that color placed on the board wins.
Victory Conditions
    The winner is the player with the biggest connection
Defeat Conditions
    Your program does not provide a command in the allotted time or it provides an unrecognized/invalid command.

  Technical Details

  • Chips are initialized to cellAmount / playerAmount(2) * colorsPerPlayer(2) rounded down to the first multiple of (2 * 2)
  • You can check out the source code of this game on this GitHub repo.

  Game Protocol

Initialization Input
First line: numberOfCells equals 37.
Next numberOfCells lines: 8 space-separated integers:
  • index for the cell's index.
  • richness for its richness.
  • 6 neigh variables: Ignore for this league.
  • 6 neigh variables, one for each direction, containing the index of a neighboring cell or -1 is there is no neighbor.
Input for One Game Turn
First line: An integer day: the current day. Equals 0 for this league.
the current day, from 0 to 5.
the current day, from 0 to 23.
Next line: An integer nutrients: the current nutrient value of the forest.
Next line: 2 space-separated integers:
  • mySun: your current sun points.
  • myScore: your current score.
Next line: 3 space-separated integers:
  • oppSun: your opponent's sun points.
  • oppScore: your opponent's score.
  • oppIsWaiting: equals 1 if your opponent is asleep, 0 otherwise.
Next line: An integer numberOfTrees for the current number of trees in the forest.
Next numberOfTrees lines: 4 space-separated integers to describe each tree:
  • cellIndex: the index of the cell this tree is on.
  • size: the size of the tree. From 0 (seed) to 3 (large tree).
  • isMine: 1 if you are the owner of this tree, 0 otherwise.
  • isDormant: Ignore in this league. 1 if this tree is dormant, 0 otherwise.
Next line: An integer numberOfPossibleActions for the number of legal moves you can make this turn.
Next numberOfPossibleActions lines: A string possibleAction containing one of the actions you can output this turn.
This list is provided to help you get started.
Output
A single line with your command:
  • GROW index: make your tree on cell index grow by 1 size.
  • SEED index0 index1: make your tree on cell index0 launch a seed onto cell index1.
  • COMPLETE index: make your large tree on the specified cell complete its lifecycle. This removes the tree.
  • WAIT: go to sleep. WAIT: go to sleep until the start of the next day.
